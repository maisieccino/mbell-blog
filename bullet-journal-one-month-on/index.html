Bullet Journal, One Month Later

Posted on 28 January 2016

Comments

At the start of the month, I made a Bullet Journal (more information here) and have been using it since. The premise has been reasonably simple. Need to remember something or make a task? It goes into the journal.

Am I More Organised?

Uh... no.

The problem is, whilst the bullet journal is nice to put notes and things in, there needs to be work on your side too. I keep slipping into the habit of making mental reminders rather than writing it down somewhere, so perhaps it'll just take some getting used to.

I already use Cortana/Siri/Google Now. How does this stack up?

I own a Windows Phone, which comes with the Cortana virtual assistant. I like it a lot, and it does automate a lot of things like setting reminders based on location or person, rather than a time (for instance, "remind me when I'm at the supermarket", "remind me next time I get a phone call from Grandma"). However, it does feel a little intrusive, especially as it can be set to trawl your emails for flight tickets or parcel tracking numbers. It's pretty neat, but a tad creepy. A notebook doesn't do any of this, you only share with it what you write in it.

Also, a notebook is cross platform.
