HackLondon, The Bestest Hackathon In The Bestest City?

Posted on 29 February 2016

Comments

This last weekend, two hundred eager hackers (me being one of them) descended upon University College London's North and South Cloisters to build, program and hack entertaining, interesting and overall very cool things.

In our team, we created a Git automation tool which we called Git++ (source, devpost). What did it do, you may ask? We forked the very popular git-plus package for Atom, and relentlessly hacked it to provide gesture control using the LEAP Motion controller - swipe upwards with your hand to commit your changes, swipe left to pull from your remote, and swipe right to push. It was a lot of fun to play with, and not so fun to develop (Sleep? What's that?), but it was the first hackathon project I've actually managed to complete, so I'm incredibly proud of what we've done! Also, we have a demo on Periscope.

There was definitely a novelty in hacking in the place you visit every day, but that sadly wore off when I realised that I'd be seeing the same place again on Monday. Such is life. It's interesting to note that the lecture theatre where we have theory lectures was used for filming in the film Inception (shout out to my man Leo Dicaps).

It's probably worth at this point giving a shout out to the legendary volunteers from UCL Tech Soc (the baes) and KCL Tech for actually making this event happen, as well as the team from MLH for also helping to support the event, as well of course the sponsors who basically pay for it all. Also, thanks to other hackers who came over to help us with a couple of bits, in return for us coming over and helping them with stuff. That's what makes hackathons so special.

Now, I should go back to bed. Ten hours of sleep for the whole weekend does not a happy Matthew make.

P.S. I'll post the results of the sleep survey as soon as possible!
