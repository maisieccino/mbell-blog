Hey, Look At That, I'm An Android User Now

Posted on 14 February 2016

Comments

About four years ago, I was in the market for my next phone. I was after something mid-range, good value, a decent camera, and above all, a good OS. I was instantly wowed by the Windows Phone 7's inky black, silky smooth interface, and picked up a Nokia Lumia 800, rather than pick an average Android device. Android at the time was not very smooth to use on a lot of devices, and most manufacturers liked to add tonnes of bloatware onto their devices (the old TouchWiz being the most infamous of them all).

Windows Phone 7 users got a bit shafted though, when we found out that we wouldn't be getting an upgrade to Windows Phone 8. At this point my contract was due again. Should I pick a budget-to-midrange Android device, or Lumia 1020 which was being offered to me at a deal? I picked Windows again (for worse perhaps), since I wasn't that content with using a half-arsed low spec Samsung. The phone ran okay for most of the time, but since late last year started developing problems and even led me to missing lectures and other things. So, a couple of weeks ago, I decided to give Android a proper chance.

I picked up the Motorola Moto G (3rd Generation) during a sale for Â£89 (RRP is Â£149), which was an absolute steal. It came with Android Lollipop (5.1) and has totally blown me away with how smooth the operating system has become. Even though the device is a tad underpowered (Snapdragon 410 and 1 GB RAM, anyone?), it can still run most things with ease and performs sublimely for what I was going to consider as a stop-gap phone to my next contract. The device feels excellent to hold, and the display is nice and bright - although it's "only" a 1280x720 pixel resolution. The battery life also lasts me a day, a far cry from the Lumia where I had to semi-permanently attach a USB cable to it.

My only issues with the device so far is the lack of storage; the Moto G only has 8 GB of onboard memory and this is half consumed by Android itself, not leaving you with loads of room. Luckily, it does have a MicroSD card slot (also something new for me as Windows Phone never used to support this) so the memory is expandable by up to 32 GB I believe. Also, the Moto G does lack an NFC chip, although this isn't really an issue for me as most of the time it's just a battery drain and a nuisance.

Droid on!
